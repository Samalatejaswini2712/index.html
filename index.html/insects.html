About Insects

  • Insects have six legs and a body divided into head, thorax, and abdomen.
  • Most insects have two antennae and compound eyes.
  • Some insects, like bees and butterflies, help in pollination.
  • Many insects undergo metamorphosis during their life cycle.
  • Insects use their mouthparts to eat, bite, or suck depending on the species.

Butterfly

Butterflies are beautiful insects known for their large, colorful wings covered with tiny scales. They feed on nectar from flowers using a long, coiled proboscis and play an important role in pollination. Butterflies undergo a fascinating life cycle with four stages: egg, caterpillar, pupa, and adult. Some species, like the Monarch butterfly, migrate over long distances in search of suitable habitats.

Honeybee

Honey bees are social insects that live in large colonies, working together to build hives, collect nectar, and produce honey. They play a vital role in pollination, helping plants grow and produce fruits and seeds. A honey bee colony has a queen, worker bees, and drones, each with specific roles. Honey bees communicate with each other through a unique "waggle dance" to share the location of food sources.

Grasshopper

Grasshoppers are insects known for their powerful hind legs that help them jump long distances. They mostly eat grass and leaves, making them herbivores. Grasshoppers have antennae to sense their surroundings and make chirping sounds by rubbing their legs against their wings. While they are an important part of nature, in large numbers they can harm crops.
